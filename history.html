Recipe History

Every recipe carries traces of the people, places, and stories that shaped it. Here we highlight where a few of the site’s featured dishes come from and why they matter.

Want to see a region featured? Use the Recipe Request page to submit a dish and its story.

India
Dal Makhani — Indian Butter Lentils
Originating in the Punjabi region, Dal Makhani became popular in North Indian restaurants in the 20th century. Slow-simmered black lentils and butter create its signature richness.
Morocco
Moroccan Spiced Chickpea Stew
Heavily influenced by Moorish, Berber, and Arab traditions, Moroccan stews combine preserved lemons, warm spices, and chickpeas for depth and brightness.
Thailand
Thai Green Curry
Green curry (kaeng khiao wan) blends fresh green chiles, herbs, and aromatics into a fragrant curry paste, balanced by coconut milk. It showcases the Thai preference for layered flavors — sweet, salty, sour, and spicy.
Ethiopia
Misir Wot — Red Lentil Stew
Centered around berbere spice and clarified butter (niter kibbeh), Misir Wot is a staple of Ethiopian cuisine and communal meals.
Caribbean
Jerk Flavors — Caribbean
Jerk seasoning evolved from indigenous and African influences; bold spices like allspice and Scotch bonnet peppers create a unique heat and aroma.
